Quantum Mechanics · Volume 1

Notation and Symbols

This table collects the mathematical symbols used throughout this book, together with the meaning each one carries here. A few letters do double duty — for example, \(T\) is a temperature in Chapter 1 but a transmission coefficient in Chapter 6, and \(\sigma\) is a standard deviation in most chapters but the name of a Pauli matrix in Chapter 9. Where that happens, both meanings are listed, and the surrounding text always makes clear which is intended.

Fundamental constants

Symbol Meaning
\(h\) Planck’s constant, \(6.626\times10^{-34}\ \mathrm{J\,s}\)
\(\hbar\) reduced Planck constant, \(\hbar = h/2\pi\)
\(k_B\) Boltzmann’s constant, \(1.381\times10^{-23}\ \mathrm{J/K}\) — the conversion factor between temperature and energy
\(c\) speed of light in vacuum, \(2.998\times10^{8}\ \mathrm{m/s}\)
\(e\) elementary charge (the magnitude of the electron’s charge)
\(m\) mass of the particle (usually the electron)

Energies, frequencies, and lengths

Symbol Meaning
\(E\) energy; \(E_n\) is the energy of the \(n\)-th allowed state
\(T\) absolute temperature, in kelvin (Chapter 1)
\(V(x)\) potential energy as a function of position
\(\nu\) frequency — oscillations per second, in hertz
\(\omega\) angular frequency, \(\omega = 2\pi\nu\)
\(\lambda\) wavelength
\(k\) wavenumber, \(k = 2\pi/\lambda\); fixes the momentum through \(p = \hbar k\)
\(\kappa\) decay rate of the wave function inside a classically forbidden region
\(L\) width of a well or region
\(a\) a characteristic width or length (e.g. the width of a well)
\(u(\nu,T)\) spectral energy density — radiant energy per unit volume per unit frequency
\(\Phi\) work function — the energy needed to free an electron from a metal (Chapter 1)

Position, momentum, and time

Symbol Meaning
\(x\) position in one dimension; \(y, z\) for additional coordinates
\(t\) time
\(p\) momentum
\(v\) velocity; \(v_g\) is the group velocity of a wave packet

Wave functions and quantum states

Symbol Meaning
\(\psi(x)\) wave function — the (often time-independent) state of the system
\(\Psi(x,t)\) the full, time-dependent wave function
\(\lvert\psi\rvert^2\) probability density — probability per unit length of finding the particle
\(\phi,\ \varphi\) a phase, or an individual basis state
\(n\) quantum number — a non-negative integer labeling the allowed states
\(\alpha,\ \beta\) the complex amplitudes of the two states of a qubit (Chapter 9)
\(A,\ B,\ C\) constants fixed by normalization or boundary conditions
\(\langle x\rangle\) expectation (average) value of a quantity — here, position
\(\sigma\) standard deviation — the spread, or uncertainty, of a quantity; also the Pauli spin matrices \(\sigma_x,\sigma_y,\sigma_z\) (Chapter 9)
\(\Delta x,\ \Delta p\) the uncertainty (standard deviation) in position and in momentum; likewise \(\Delta\sigma_x\) for a Pauli observable

Operators

Symbol Meaning
\(\hat H\) Hamiltonian — the total-energy operator
\(\hat x,\ \hat p\) the position and momentum operators
\(\hat a,\ \hat a^\dagger\) the lowering and raising (ladder) operators of the harmonic oscillator
\(\dagger\) Hermitian conjugate (adjoint) of an operator
\(H_n(\xi)\) Hermite polynomial of order \(n\), with \(\xi = \sqrt{m\omega/\hbar}\,x\) (harmonic-oscillator states)

Coefficients and probabilities

Symbol Meaning
\(R\) reflection coefficient — the probability a particle bounces back from a barrier
\(T\) transmission coefficient — the probability it passes through (Chapter 6)
\(P\) probability

Mathematical notation

Symbol Meaning
\(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial x}\) partial derivative with respect to \(x\)
\(\langle\,\cdot\mid\cdot\,\rangle\) inner product of two states
\(\delta_{mn}\) Kronecker delta — equals \(1\) if \(m=n\), and \(0\) otherwise
\(i\) the imaginary unit, \(i^2 = -1\)
\(\infty\) infinity
\(\displaystyle\int\) integral — sums a quantity over a region or over all space
\(\displaystyle\sum\) summation over a set of states
