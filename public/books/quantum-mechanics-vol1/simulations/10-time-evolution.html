Time evolution: unitary vs unstable

A Gaussian wave packet evolving under the time-dependent Schrödinger equation. The Crank–Nicolson stepper is exactly unitary — the norm holds at 1.000 forever. Switch to explicit Euler and watch the normalization indicator climb past 1 within a few steps and the packet blow up: explicit Euler is unconditionally unstable for the Schrödinger equation, the chapter's central warning made visible.

Crank–Nicolson: norm conserved at machine precision regardless of step size. Try the barrier (set k₀ high enough and watch part of the packet tunnel through) and the well. Then switch to Euler to see why the stepper choice is not optional.
