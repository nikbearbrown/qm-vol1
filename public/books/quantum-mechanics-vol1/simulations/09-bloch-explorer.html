Bloch-sphere explorer

Every pure qubit state is a point on the sphere: |ψ⟩ = cos(θ/2)|0⟩ + eiφ sin(θ/2)|1⟩. Drag the polar angle θ and the azimuth φ and watch the state vector, its components ⟨σx⟩, ⟨σy⟩, ⟨σz⟩, and the Born-rule probabilities for measuring each Pauli observable.

North pole is |0⟩ (σz = +1), south is |1⟩ (σz = −1), and the equator holds the equal superpositions. The global phase is invisible; only the relative phase φ matters, and it shows up in ⟨σx⟩ and ⟨σy⟩. A measurement axis gives a certain outcome exactly when the state sits on that axis.
