A single eigenstate is stationary: its density stands still and ⟨x⟩ is flat. Motion needs at least two different energies. With only ψ₁ and ψ₂, ⟨x⟩ oscillates at the beat frequency (E₂−E₁)/ℏ = 3. Because every En = n² is an integer here, the whole state exactly revives after t = 2π. Adding higher states with their faster phases makes the density break up and re-form in more intricate ways — yet ⟨H⟩ = ∑|cn|²En / ∑|cn|² stays pinned.
