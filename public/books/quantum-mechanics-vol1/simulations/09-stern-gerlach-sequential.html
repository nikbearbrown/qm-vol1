Sequential Stern–Gerlach

A spin-up beam |0⟩ enters a chain of Stern–Gerlach analysers. Each one measures spin along its axis, splitting the beam; you keep one output and pass it on. The relative beam intensities are exact Born probabilities. Set the chain to Z → X → Z and watch the middle X erase the original Z certainty — the final Z comes out 50/50.

Presets:

Intensities are relative to the entering |0⟩ beam (set to 1). A keep-toggle collapses the state onto that eigenstate before the next analyser; the final analyser shows both of its outputs. Because |+x⟩ and |−x⟩ are each a 50/50 mix of |0⟩ and |1⟩, any X stage between two Z stages wipes out the Z memory: [σx,σz] ≠ 0 made tangible.
