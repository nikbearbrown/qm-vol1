The de Broglie wavelength

Every moving particle has a wavelength λ = h/p. Pick a particle and its energy, and watch where the wavelength falls — and whether it is large enough to diffract from a crystal.

Non-relativistic: p = √(2mK), λ = h/p, with h = 6.626×10⁻³⁴ J·s. For a singly-charged particle the kinetic energy in electron-volts equals the accelerating voltage in volts. The shaded band marks the 0.05–0.3 nm spacing between atomic planes in a crystal — diffraction is observable only when λ lands in or near it.
