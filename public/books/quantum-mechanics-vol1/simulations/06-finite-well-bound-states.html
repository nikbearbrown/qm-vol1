Finite-well bound states

The bound states of a finite square well are the crossings of a quarter-circle of radius z₀ with the tangent (even) and cotangent (odd) branches, where z₀ = (L/2ℏ)√(2mV₀) sets the well's depth×width. Slide z₀ and watch states appear from the top as the well deepens or widens — and notice that one state always survives.

A new bound state appears every time z₀ passes a multiple of π/2, alternating even, odd, even, odd, … The count is ⌈2z₀/π⌉. However small z₀ becomes, the quarter-circle still meets the first tangent branch, so a finite well — no matter how shallow — always holds at least one bound state. (At exactly z₀ = nπ/2 a new state sits right at threshold, E → 0.)
