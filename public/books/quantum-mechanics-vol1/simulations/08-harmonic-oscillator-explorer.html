The energy eigenstates of V(x) = ½mω²x². Pick a level n: the wave function ψn (or the density |ψn|²) is drawn on its rung En = (n+½)ℏω inside the parabola. Dashed lines mark the classical turning points; the shaded tails beyond them are the classically forbidden region. Dimensionless units ξ = √(mω/ℏ)·x, energies in ℏω.

The spacing is uniform — every rung sits ℏω above the one below — and the ground state floats at E₀ = ½ℏω, never zero. Notice ψn has exactly n nodes, the turning points widen as √(2n+1), and a slice of probability always leaks past them: about 16% for n=0, shrinking as n grows and the state turns classical.
