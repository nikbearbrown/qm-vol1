Group velocity vs. phase velocity

A free electron wave packet. The envelope — the particle — moves at the group velocity vg; the crests inside it move at the phase velocity vph = vg/2. The envelope outruns its own ripples, so each crest drifts backward through the packet. The packet also slowly spreads.

For a free non-relativistic particle ω = ℏk²/2m, so vph = ℏk/2m and vg = ℏk/m — the group velocity is exactly twice the phase velocity and equals the classical speed p/m. Raising k₀ speeds both up (ratio stays 2); a wider packet spreads more slowly. Velocities are shown in arbitrary units (1.00 = the default k₀).
