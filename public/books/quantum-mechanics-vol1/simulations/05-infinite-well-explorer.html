The energies climb as n², so the gaps widen as you go up the ladder. Doubling the box width quarters every level (E ∝ 1/L²); a heavier particle lowers the whole ladder (E ∝ 1/m). The state ψn has exactly n−1 interior nodes and carries momentum p = nπℏ/L. Room-temperature thermal energy is about 0.025 eV — compare it with E1 to see whether confinement effects dominate.
