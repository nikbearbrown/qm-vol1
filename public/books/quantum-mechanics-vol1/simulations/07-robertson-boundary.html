The Gaussian rides exactly on the bound; the infinite-square-well states sit strictly above it, and climbing the ladder in n drives the point further away. For the well, σx = L√(1/12 − 1/(2n²π²)) and σp = nπℏ/L, so the ratio is 2nπ√(1/12 − 1/(2n²π²)). It equals 1.136 at n = 1 and grows like (π/√3)·n ≈ 1.81n for large n — the position spread saturates at L/√12 while the momentum spread keeps rising. The Gaussian gives σx = a/√2, σp = 1/(a√2), a product of exactly ℏ/2 for every a.
