Coherent states & oscillator dynamics

Watch |Ψ(x,t)|² evolve in the harmonic well. A single eigenstate holds perfectly still. A two-state superposition sloshes at the classical frequency ω. A coherent state |α⟩ is the minimum-uncertainty Gaussian that rides the classical orbit forever without spreading — the quantum state of laser light. Units ξ = √(mω/ℏ)·x, ℏ = ω = 1, so the period is 2π.

The energy ⟨H⟩ is constant in every case — even while the density sloshes — because each eigenstate's phase rotates at its own rate and only the cross terms move |Ψ|². The eigenstate's |Ψ|² is exactly time-independent (the rotating phase cancels). The coherent state keeps σxσp = ℏ/2 for all time and traces ⟨x⟩(t) = √2·α·cos t: a classical orbit carried out by a quantum packet that never disperses.
