Electron interference diffraction

Electrons arrive one at a time, each a single dot at a spot no one can predict — yet after thousands of them an interference pattern appears. Only one electron is ever in the apparatus at a time.

Each dot is drawn from the probability density |ψ|² for a wave that passed through both slits: a cosine-squared fringe pattern under a single-slit envelope. The electrons never interact — each interferes only with itself. The run starts automatically and stops at 30,000 electrons; use Pause to freeze it, Reset to start over, and the slider to change the fringe spacing.
