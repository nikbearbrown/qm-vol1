Potential step: reflection & transmission

A particle arrives from the left at a step of height V₀. The real part of the wave function is shown: a standing interference pattern builds on the left from the incident and reflected waves, while on the right the wave either travels on (E > V₀) or decays as an evanescent tail (E < V₀). Even with energy to spare, some reflection always remains.

For E < V₀ the reflection is total (R = 1), yet an evanescent tail still penetrates the step — probability is present there, but no current flows through. For E > V₀ transmission turns on, but R stays above zero: any abrupt change in the wavelength reflects part of the wave, just like an impedance mismatch on a transmission line. Reflection vanishes only when the step disappears.
