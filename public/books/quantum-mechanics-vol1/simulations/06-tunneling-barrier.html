Tunneling barrier explorer

An electron of energy E meets a rectangular barrier of height V₀ and width L. The real part of the wave function is found by integrating the Schrödinger equation exactly. Watch it oscillate, decay across the barrier, and emerge with reduced amplitude — and see how steeply the transmission collapses as the barrier widens.

Below the barrier (E < V₀) the wave decays as e−κx and T is exponentially small; the WKB estimate e−2κL tracks it closely. Add one ångström of width and watch T fall by roughly a factor of e2κ. Above the barrier (E > V₀) the curve oscillates everywhere and T → 1 at resonances, where the barrier width fits a whole number of half-wavelengths.
