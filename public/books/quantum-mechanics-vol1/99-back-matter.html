A Note on the Index

This book has no index, and the omission is deliberate. This is a Kindle and online edition with reflowable text: there are no fixed pages, so a page-anchored index would point at nothing. Full-text search already does what a traditional index did — instantly, and for every word rather than a curated few. More importantly, this book is designed for integration with Medhavy (medhavy.com), Humanitarians AI’s intelligent-textbook platform, named from the Sanskrit मेधावी, “intellectually brilliant.” Medhavy provides semantic lookup — ask for a concept, not just a keyword — along with adaptive navigation through the book’s structure. Between search and Medhavy, a static index would be the least capable tool on the shelf.

Glossary

Wave function (ψ) — The complex-valued function that completely describes the state of a quantum system; everything measurable about the system is computed from it.

Probability density (|ψ|²) — The squared modulus of the wave function, giving probability per unit length; it must be integrated over a region to yield an actual probability.

Born rule — The rule that the probability of finding a particle in an interval is the integral of |ψ|² over that interval.

Normalization — The requirement that the total probability of finding the particle somewhere equals exactly 1.

Superposition — A quantum state formed by adding two or more states together, which behaves as all of its components at once until measured.

Schrödinger equation — The fundamental equation of motion of quantum mechanics, telling the wave function how to change in time.

Stationary state — An energy eigenstate whose probability density never changes in time; only an overall complex phase rotates.

Eigenvalue and eigenstate — A special state that an operator merely rescales, and the number it rescales it by; measurement outcomes are eigenvalues.

Boundary condition — A requirement the wave function must satisfy at the edge of a region, such as vanishing at an impenetrable wall.

Quantization — The restriction of a physical quantity, such as energy, to a discrete set of allowed values, arising from confinement and boundary conditions.

Ground state — The lowest-energy state available to a system; in quantum mechanics its energy is generally above the bottom of the potential.

Zero-point energy — The irreducible minimum energy a confined quantum system retains even in its ground state.

Tunneling — The passage of a particle through a barrier that classical mechanics says it lacks the energy to cross, because the wave function decays but never quite reaches zero inside the barrier.

Transmission coefficient — The probability that a particle incident on a barrier or step passes through rather than reflecting.

Ladder operators — The raising and lowering operators of the harmonic oscillator, which move a state up or down the energy ladder by one quantum.

Wave packet — A localized, normalizable state built by superposing plane waves of nearby momenta.

Group velocity — The speed at which a wave packet’s envelope, and hence the particle’s likely position, travels.

Dispersion — The spreading of a wave packet over time, caused by its component waves traveling at different speeds.

Operator — A mathematical rule that turns one wave function into another; in quantum mechanics every observable quantity is represented by one.

Hermitian operator — An operator equal to its own adjoint, guaranteeing real eigenvalues and therefore real measurement outcomes.

Expectation value — The average of many measurements of an observable on identically prepared systems.

Commutator — The difference AB minus BA between two operators; when it is nonzero, the two observables cannot both be sharp in the same state.

Uncertainty principle — The theorem that the spreads of position and momentum in any state satisfy σₓσₚ ≥ ℏ/2; a property of states, not of clumsy instruments.

Measurement collapse — The state-update rule: immediately after a measurement returns an eigenvalue, the system is in the corresponding eigenstate.

Qubit — A two-level quantum system, the unit of quantum information; its general state is a superposition of |0⟩ and |1⟩.

Photon — The quantum of light, carrying energy hν and momentum h/λ.

de Broglie wavelength — The wavelength λ = h/p associated with any particle of momentum p, making matter itself wavelike.
